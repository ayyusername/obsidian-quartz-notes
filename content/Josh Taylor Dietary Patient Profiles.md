**Overview**

This plan is developed for two patients aiming for weight loss through a whole foods vegan diet, structured macronutrient distributions, intermittent fasting, and tracking mechanisms to monitor progress. They support each other as accountability partners.

**1. Individual Profiles and Calorie Targets**

**Patient A**

- **Age:** 31
- **Gender:** Male
- **Height:** 5’10”
- **Weight:** 180 lbs
- **BMI:** 25.8 (Overweight)
- **Health Conditions:** Familial Hypercholesterolemia (FH), fatty liver, hypertension
- **Lifestyle:** Limited physical activity with daily slow walks
- **Dietary Preferences:** Primarily vegan with occasional Greek yogurt and cheese; high-fiber, plant-based meals
- **Goals:** Lose weight, manage cholesterol, improve liver health, reduce blood pressure
- **Monitoring Needs:** Regular blood pressure checks; periodic cholesterol and liver function tests

**Patient B**

- **Age:** 35
- **Gender:** Male
- **Height:** 6’0”
- **Weight:** 210 lbs
- **BMI:** 28.5 (Overweight)
- **Health Conditions:** Vascular Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome (vEDS), fatty liver, hypertension, severe asthma
- **Lifestyle:** Limited physical activity with daily slow walks
- **Dietary Preferences:** Primarily vegan with occasional Greek yogurt and cheese; high-protein, anti-inflammatory meals
- **Goals:** Lose weight, improve liver health, reduce blood pressure
- **Monitoring Needs:** Regular blood pressure checks; periodic liver function tests

**2. Shared Health Challenges and Dietary Strategies**

**Fatty Liver and Hypertension Management**

- Control total calorie intake
- Eliminate alcohol consumption
- Emphasize whole, nutrient-dense foods
- Reduce sodium and increase potassium-rich foods (leafy greens, legumes)
- Incorporate healthy fats (e.g., avocados, nuts, seeds)

**Condition-Specific Dietary Focus**

- **For FH (Patient A):** High intake of soluble fibers, plant sterols, and limited saturated fats
- **For vEDS (Patient B):** Adequate protein, anti-inflammatory foods (leafy greens, berries, omega-3-rich seeds)
- **For Severe Asthma (Patient B):** Immune-supportive foods (antioxidants, flax seeds); avoid dietary triggers

**3. Macronutrient Distributions and Calorie Breakdown**

**Macronutrient Ratios (Moderate Carbohydrate Plan)**

- **Protein:** 30%
- **Fat:** 25%
- **Carbohydrates:** 45%

**Targets per Patient:**

- **Patient A:** 1500 kcal (∼113 g protein, ∼42 g fat, ∼169 g carbs)
- **Patient B:** 1800 kcal (∼135 g protein, ∼50 g fat, ∼203 g carbs)

**4. Dietary Emphasis for Both Patients**

1. **High-Fiber Carbohydrates:** Lentils, beans, quinoa, oats, sweet potatoes
2. **Vegan Protein Sources:** Tofu, tempeh, seitan, legumes, chickpea flour, occasional Greek yogurt/cheese
3. **Healthy Fats:** Flax seeds, nuts, seeds, avocados, minimal olive oil
4. **Minimal Sodium and Added Sugars:** Supports blood pressure and cardiometabolic health
5. **Fiber Intake Goal:** 25-30 g daily from legumes, vegetables, grains, and flax seeds

**5. Weight Loss Goals and Strategies**

**Healthy Weight Loss Rate:** 1-2 lbs/week (12-24 lbs over 12 weeks)

**Strategies:**

1. Maintain caloric deficit (1500 kcal for Patient A, 1800 kcal for Patient B)
2. Use intermittent fasting (16/8 method)
3. Adopt sustainable habits to avoid muscle loss and nutrient deficiencies
