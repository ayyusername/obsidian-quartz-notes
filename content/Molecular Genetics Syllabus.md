### Course Description

The basic principles and methodologies of molecular biology and genetics are applied to genome organization, replication, expression, regulation, mutation, and reorganization. Emphasis will be placed on a broad introduction to and integration of important topics in prokaryotic and eukaryotic systems.  
**Prerequisite**: Biol 310 or consent of the instructor.

---

### Purpose of the Course and Learning Outcomes

This course is designed as an advanced course in molecular biology, defined as the study of genes and their activities at the molecular level. Molecular biology is a complex field with relevance and applications to all areas of biology. Our understanding of molecular biology principles advanced tremendously during the 20th century, culminating with the sequencing of the human genome, but many intriguing questions are still unanswered. The molecular biologists of the future will be asking fascinating questions about a complex and fascinating field. Following a discovery approach, the course will introduce you to key concepts in the field using experimental evidence.

In this course, you will develop problem-solving skills and demonstrate mastery of the subject by applying what you have learned to new situations.

As a student in this class, you will:

- Understand the scientific process in the context of learning the fundamental concepts of molecular biology and the central dogma.
- Demonstrate knowledge of the multiple layers of regulation involved in the flow of genetic information.
- Learn the mechanisms and regulation of genome maintenance and gene expression.
- Learn to recognize and evaluate the impact of molecular structure modifications/changes on biological systems and potential evolutionary consequences.
- Learn the latest technologies in genomics.

### Teaching Philosophy

My goal is to make the course relevant, interesting, and informative by actively engaging you in the learning process. The course describes the flow of information from genes to proteins following the metaphor of the dogma of molecular biology. Molecular biology is an experimental science, and its concepts become meaningful as you apply them to the knowledge you already have. I believe that scientific concepts are learned best when you understand the experimental design and the data that led to those conclusions instead of just memorizing the facts. In this course, we will study molecular biology concepts by understanding the connections between the experiments and those concepts. The activities and assignments of the course are designed to help you master the foundational knowledge required to apply, analyze, and evaluate scientific research.

### Expectations and Preparation for Course Success

Biol 540 requires participation in discussions and activities. By enrolling in this course, you joined a community of learners. What happens during class adds to your learning experience beyond what you learn from studying in solitude. Active participation and attendance are strongly encouraged. We will be working in groups to develop a sense of community. Attend and be prepared for class by reading and completing all assigned work in advance. Please use the syllabus to pace your reading of the textbook and completing the assignments. Knowledge is cumulative. Assignments are due in specific order. Respecting the due dates keeps you focused and on schedule. Provide and consider constructive evaluations and feedback.

Online quizzes are provided to offer students the opportunity to review the background to the concepts presented in class and in readings. These quizzes are available before class. Online quizzes will emphasize multiple choice- and multiple answer-style questions which offer the advantage of providing immediate feedback. Online quizzes are open book, and students will commonly be allowed multiple attempts to take the quiz, so these are essentially credit/no credit exercises. Online exercises are designed to be concise, painless, and focus your attention on key principles or questions that have or will come up in class discussion. Please also remember that quizzes are an opportunity to check and receive feedback about your grasp of key class concepts before you sit for examination. From the time a quiz is posted, students will typically have 4 days or longer to complete the quiz. Please have some patience with your instructor regarding the timing of when the quizzes appear online; each quiz takes time to prepare.

### Tips for Success

- Arrive to class on time. This is an interactive, discussion-based course, so your participation is essential to your success.
- Ask questions early and often. If something in class isn't clear to you, it's always the case that you are not the only one confused. Your classmates will thank you for asking for clarification.
- Complete the quizzes and problem assignments on time. These exercises are designed to focus your attention on key principles or questions that will direct class discussion.
- It is highly encouraged to visit during office hours.
- Take frequent written notes and draw your own diagrams. There is no substitute for the learning power of creating your own notes.
- Your lecture notes should be a key source of study material, but it is highly recommended to also read and consult the textbook. Lecture exercises and textbook readings are complementary. Many of the diagrams on the PowerPoint lectures are from your textbook, which ensures that you will always have access to additional explanation and context as you study and review the class material. Do not feel overwhelmed by the volume of knowledge the book contains. You should treat the textbook as a reference to help you better understand the course material.

### Grading Criteria

**EXAMS**: Two mid-term exams and a final exam will be given during the semester. The exams will be in the form of problem-solving and short answers. Questions will be based on material presented in class and supported by the readings and recommended questions and problems. The tests may include multiple choice, short answers, and data analysis and interpretation. Exams must be completed in the classroom using Canvas. Anything discussed in class is fair game, even if it is not in the syllabus or in the book.

**Other**: During the semester, many points will be accumulated from assignments and/or quizzes. The accumulated points will be converted to % and the % points will determine the total number of "Other" points you get in the course (more details during the 1st class).

**NO MAKE-UP EXAMS**: If a mid-term is missed (with an approved medical excuse), 67% of the final exam points will be used as the points for the missed exam. Missing the final exam or the two mid-terms will result in failing the course. Permission for missing a mid-term exam will require a clearly written official medical note. No exam make-ups will be given due to non-academic travel conflicts, non-medically related absence, or conflict with other exams.
