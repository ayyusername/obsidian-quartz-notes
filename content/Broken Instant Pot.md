There are 3 leak points that can be recovered.

1. Lid-pot interface: make sure seal is seated and not ripped or torn. No debris or contamination
    
2. Seal-open switch. Make sure it’s centered and not in the “open” position to either side. Also check for contamination, damage, signs of wear
    
3. The bleed off valve that seals upon reaching pressurization. Same inspection. The gasket ring on the inside of the floating bobber, check it for signs of wear or damage.
    

If those are okay and you still hear a leak, visually inspect where steam may be escaping. If it’s not one of those 3 points, time to retire the bad boy.
