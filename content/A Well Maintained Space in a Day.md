# A Well Maintained Space in a Day

Created: 2024-05-30 13:19

Here I shall record and synthesize my varied ideas on how to implement routines by virtue of habit stacking that will ritualize as many elements of home and life as possible to achieve maintenance largely devoid of reactive executive functioning, with the goal of preserving that functioning for other purposes. 

My house would be pretty much reset in the morning and evening. There is an acceptable amount of clutter, but I think I'd like to designate "clutter" spaces where a little chaos and clutter are expected and anticipated by my mind, and they would contrast pretty heavily with the surrounding reset spaces. 

The goal would be to maintain spaces to a degree insofar as they can be tidied to company-presentable. Like, the whole apartment quickly tidied in like 15-20 minutes. That also means that the morning and evening resets shouldn't take more than 15-20 minutes at most!

I estimate 20 minutes because I don't think I'd need more than 5-10 minutes for my things--his would likely be much further down the line. 

Don't set it down, put it away! What doesn't have a place and needs a place?

Example: 

Taylor knows he needs 10 minutes to tidy up behind himself before he leaves, and also knows he has a meeting in an hour. Taylor would decide when to leave to both make his meeting, get ready to leave, and "clean up his crumbs."

What does tidying up your crumbs look like, dear taylor? 

1. As you put things away, have they been put away completely and neatly? Are any movable pieces of furniture returned to resting state? Consider -- are you doing this in a rush, or mindfully? 
2. Walk around the living room. Are there stray socks or clothes? Are any pillows moved by you moved back in place? Have you reset the weed table? Are there any bev containers that need to move to coffee stand or dishwasher? Do the lights need to be turned off because you're the last person leaving the room? 
3. Walk around the kitchen. Are there stray items among the food items that could be put away, or into your drawer? Have any dishes you've used been put into the dishwasher or been hand washed? Are there any dishes on the drying rack that are dry and could be put away, particularly any items you most recently used drying? If you've taken out the trash or recycling, have you replaced the bags and nearly returned the receptacles to their position under the island? Are any dish sponges/soap you've pulled out that need to return to under the sink? If you used a surface, did you wipe it down? 
6. In the pantry, are all of your shoes and any shoes jostled out by you returned neatly? Has any dirt spilled on the floor been cleaned up, and audrey's water refreshed if soiled by shoes? 
7. In the bedroom, has the bed been made tidily? Are any clothes that are folded and clean either put in their drawers, or your set of houseclothes put completely in the little basket? Have the couple of remaining bedroom items been put away tidily, and your nightstand cleared? Are the curtains either all the way open or all the way closed? 
8. In the bathroom, is the toilet seat down? Have any pee spots you've created been cleaned up? Are towels left behind unbunched? Has the shower curtain been closed and the floor mats returned? If you've cut facial hair, head hair, or nails, is there evidence of that anywhere outside of the trashcan? Has any evidence of sink used been rinsed down the sink? Have all personal care items and tools been returned to where they were acquired from? 
9. In the backroom, is the seat pushed into under the desk? Have the blinds been closed? Is the desk reset? Do you have any stray items to organize?

Step 0: Identify a goal... visualize a clear outcome. This probably includes a feeling, a vibe, perhaps something quantifyable too. What does success look and feel like? How can it be measured? 
Step one: Backcast the steps to get there
: Order those steps into a plan with measurable outcomes. 
Step two: schedule the items of the plan with a frequency that maintains adequate status with minimum attention. Do this into some kind of template sheet or something, that can be used! Include when measurements will be taken, and reviewed!
