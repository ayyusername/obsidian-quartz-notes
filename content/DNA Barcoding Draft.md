##### Methods 

DNA Extraction + PCR

The DNA extraction protocol was optimized for exuviae, previously demonstrated to contain substantially lower concentrations of free DNA (citation). Potential Aphonopelma hentzi molts were submitted by collaborators at the University of Missouri, St. Louis, in **these conditions**, liberated of visible fangs and pedipalps to minimize chances of prey DNA contamination, and placed into labeled centrifuge tubes. The tube recieved 350 uL RL Buffer, followed by 50 uL Proteinase K Stock, incubated under agitation in a water shaker bath at 65* C for 2 hours, and allowed to cool to room temperature. An additional 50 uL PKS was added and tube was stored at room temperature for 48 hours. Molts were further disrupted with a pipette tip and 450 uL of RL buffer transferred to a clean tube, followed by 250 uL of Neutralization Buffer. Tubes were centrifuged for 5 minutes at **xxxxx** rpm to pelletize molt particles. **x**ml of supernatant was transferred to a spin column, centrifuged, and washed with 500mL wash buffer 3x. Spin column was then transferred to a new catch tube, and centrifuged for 2 minutes with 100uL distilled H20 to elute DNA. Eluted DNA was stored on ice until divided between 3 tubes, to each of which was added 30 uL reaction mix. 35 PCR cycles, then stored at 4C.  

Organism Sample Description

The sample material was provided to students pre-digested with Proteinase K in a capped centrifuge tube. The molt material was largely dark brown to black, containing a mix consisting primarily of what appear to be setae, leg parts, and thorax detritus, suspended in a clear liquid. The molt material's suspected donor is Aphonopelma hentzi, a medium-sized burrowing tarantula species native to the Southern United States notable for it's docile temperament and enduring lifespan of 20 years or longer in captivity. 

PCR Experiment Description; primers and target gene, cycles included



On ice, 3 separate PCR tubes received 35 uL of Reaction Mix containing CO1 primer, spider CO1 primer, and Master Mix. Mitochondrial gene CO1 encodes for Cytochrome-C-Oxygenase Subunit 1, an enzyme highly conserved in animals critical to cellular respiration via the mitochondrial electron transport chain. CO1 is the most commonly used genetic marker for animals given it's high interspecies variability, low intraspecies variability, and high number of gene copies in a single cell, yielding robust gene libraries exist for reference and sample identification. Spider-specific CO1 primer was included to filter for amplified DNA originating from prey or other contaminants. Master Mix contained Taq polymerase, MgCl2, dNTPs, and buffer. 

Each PCR tube received either 5uL of sample DNA, 5 uL of a positive control, or 5uL of negative control prior to transfer to a thermal cycler. An initial denaturation treatment of 94 *C* for 2 minutes was applied, followed by 35 3.5 minute cycles of 30 s denaturation at 94, 2 minute annealing at 55, and a 1 minute extension at 1 minute, with the final extension step held for 10 minutes. The thermal cycler temperature was lowered to 12 until samples were transferred to storage at 4C.

DNA Purification Complete Description

PCR product was purified using QIAGEN QIAquick PCR Purification Kit. 20 uL of PCR product was transferred to a labelled tube, to which was added 30 uL water and 250 uL Buffer PB. Resulting solution was transferred to a QIAquick spin column, centrifuged at top speed for 1 minute, and filtrate disposed. The spin column was washed with 750 uL Buffer PE spun at top speed for 1 minute and the filtrate disposed. The spin column was centrifuged at top speed for 1 minute to dry the filter. The dried filter was then transferred to a clean spin column, topped with elution buffer and rested one minute, then spun for 3minutes at half speed, eluting the filter-bound DNA into the tube. The spin column was disposed and tube capped. 

Gel Electrophoresis Complete Description

Extraction, amplification, and purification products were eletrophoresed to visualize contents against controls. A 2% agarose gel was prepared with 200 mL 1XTAE and 4 grams of agarose and transferred to the **electrophoresis container** containing a **1xTAE** bath. 10 uL extract, 5 uL PCR product, and 5 uL purified PCR product were transferred to 3 separate tubes. 2 uL of loading buffer and 3 uL of dH2O were added to each and thoroughly mixed. Two mw ladders, a positive control, negative control, and each of the three samples were loaded separately and run for 25 minutes at 100 v. Gels were visualized and digitally photographed under UV.
