### Course Description:  
**BIOZ 310. Laboratory in Genetics. 2 Hours**  
Semester course; 1 lecture and 3 laboratory hours. 2 credits. Prerequisites: UNIV 200 or HONR 200; and BIOL 152 and BIOZ 152, each with a minimum grade of C. Pre- or corequisite: BIOL 310. Demonstrates the laws and molecular basis of heredity through exercises and experiments that use a variety of organisms.

### Course Goals:  
- Develop critical thinking and applied genetics lab skills for careers in the biological sciences.  
- Gain experience in applying experimental design by conducting experiments and analyzing data.  
- Become adept at genetic data analysis from DNA extraction through sequence analysis and protein imaging.  
- Advance scientific writing skills and become familiar with conducting scientific literature reviews.  
- Gain exposure to various subdisciplines in genetics, learn how to make connections between them, and understand how and why various lab techniques are applied in research.

### Learning Outcomes:  
- Perform hands-on lab experiments in essential genetic lab techniques such as PCR, restriction digestion, gel electrophoresis, DNA sequencing, and sequence analysis.  
- Demonstrate skills in how to search and utilize genetic databases (NCBI Blast and Swiss Model).  
- Explain how phenotypes are controlled at the sequence level and through epigenetic mechanisms.  
- Conduct scientific literature searches and critically evaluate peer-reviewed scientific research.  
- Utilize genetic cross and three-point cross data to determine modes of inheritance for various traits and map genetic mutants to a particular chromosome and position in the genome.  
- Perform experiments using model genetic organisms and explain their role in genetic research.  
- Identify changes at the genetic level that lead to observable changes in phenotype through genetic transformation and learn how CRISPR-Cas9 is a tool of genetic transformation.

### What to Expect and Tips for Success:  
As a biology major laboratory ½ WI course, you will learn essential applied genetics lab techniques, understand how these techniques are applied in various lab experiments. Students will be exposed to working in a genetics lab setting and gain hands-on practical experience. Students will practice execution of the Scientific Method by applying experimental design, data collection, and hypothesis testing to various lab exercises. Students will develop skills in reading peer-review scientific papers and thinking critically. Students will apply their knowledge and communication skills through reflective writing assignments as well as quizzes, experiments, post labs, and exams.

### Tips for Success:  
- As a ½ WI course, you should expect to devote time to writing and conducting literature searches.  
- As a 2-credit laboratory course, you should not expect to spend less time than you do in a 3-credit biology major course.  
- Plan to attend lab and lecture every week.  
- Attend faculty student hours and preceptor review sessions.

### Course Policies:  

#### Attendance:  
Lecture: Attending the lab lecture weekly is essential to a student’s success in the course. Because students work in pairs/groups, not attending lecture impacts other students as well. Missing more than 2 lectures will result in a grade cap at a B. Missing 3 or more lectures will result in a grade cap at a C. Attendance will be recorded weekly using TopHat.

Lab: In this lab course engagement and attendance is critical to student success. All students must attend only the lab for which they are registered. A large part of student success in this course is coming to lab every week prepared to learn. Weekly lab attendance is required and will be recorded. Lab TAs will review the lab procedure at the start of lab each week, hand back graded work, and review assignments when appropriate. Students are required to attend lab every week. Missing more than two labs will result in an F in the course. Any students arriving to lab class ≥10 minutes late or leaving the lab class before the lab has ended will be recorded as “absent”. Weekly labs are unique and we are unable to accommodate make-up labs. Only in limited circumstances, attending a different lab section may be considered upon request. If you must miss a lab let your TA know as soon as possible.

This course follows principles of Universal Design, allowing limited flexibility to meet the needs of students including those with SAEO accommodations. As a hands-on experiential course attendance is mandatory. More than two absences from lab will result in an F in the course due to lack of participation. This attendance policy is applicable to all student absences that cannot be avoided (ability, illness, family emergency, other).

#### Exams:  
Exams are administered on paper during the lecture day/time. Calculators are required for exams. There are three in-person non-cumulative exams given during the lecture period. Exams will be in the format of free response short answer questions, problem solving, labeling, fill-in-the-blank, and multiple-choice questions. Proper documentation will be required for emergency situations that interfere with scheduled testing dates and should be brought to our attention as soon as possible. Do not schedule travel or other plans that conflict with exam dates. Using a phone or leaving the classroom and returning with a phone during an exam without specific approval will be treated as an honor violation and a complaint filed with the Office of Student Conduct and Academic Affairs. The course staff reserves the right to record possible instances of honor violations during exams for use as evidence if/when a formal charge is filed with the Office of Student Conduct and Academic Integrity.

#### Quizzes:  
Quizzes are administered in the first 15 minutes of lab each week. Quizzes are based on the lecture and procedure for the lab performed that week. Quizzes are timed. Two lowest lab quiz scores will be dropped at the end of the semester. There are no make-up quizzes. If you miss a quiz please review the quiz during student hours or in lab so that you can use it to study.

#### Post Labs:  
Post Labs are due at the beginning of lab each week. There is a Post Lab assignment for each lab. In most cases students should be completing the Post Lab during your scheduled lab class time so that your workload in the course is limited to your scheduled lab section time. For acceptance of late work, documentation is required. At the end of the semester, two lowest Post Lab grades are dropped. If a student misses a lab they must turn in their post lab from the previous week via email to the lab instructor prior to the start of lab.

#### Writing Assignments:  
Writing assignments allow students to develop science writing and literature search skills. Students will have one Scientific Paper writing assignment on an experiment performed in lab and two Reflection Paper writing assignments. Writing assignments will be submitted online in Canvas using TurnItIn which searches for plagiarism and honor code violations will be reported when detected. Assignments turned in late on the assigned day will be deducted 1% per hour, including weekends and holidays. Past 72 hours, no late work will be accepted without supporting documentation.
